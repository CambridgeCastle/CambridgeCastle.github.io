Cambridge Castle Mound

"Up to the past"

Stand on the top of Cambridge and have the best view of the past and present of the city. Enjoy a relaxing walk alone or spend time with your family.
Today the Castle hill is a charming park for outdoor activities. This website helps to imagine and understand the remains of the castle, while it outlines a possible development plan for a more fruitful usage. The aim of the plan is keeping balance between the understanding of the past and the everyday community use of the place.

A possible plan for the development of the castle

I am a Heriatge and Museum Studies student at the University of Cambridge. The outlined development draft is part of my assessed essay for Heritage menagement paper. The elaborated essay is available upon request.

  • Notes on the present problems:

    • Erosion
    • Not understandable space, the history of the motte and bailey is not presented or visible
    • Bad-looking solutions (e.g.stairs, information table)
    • Wrong use (e.g. skaterboards) causes more erosion

  • Aims of development:

    • Stoping erosion
    • Aesthetical rearrangment of space
    • Development for the community use which have been present so far (e.g. weddings)
    • Emphasizing 'the best view of Cambridge' idea
    • Avoiding historicism
    • Providing proper information about the sense of place: giving historical information for feeling the past while seeing the present
    • All the solutions according to the "up to the past" slogen

  • Possible solutions:

    • Ground and garden works:
      • Fixing the mound
      • Revisioning the vegetation, planned gardening work
      • Renewing the lawn
    • Architectural works for a better use of place:
      • Access: new stairs
      • Slightly lifted look-out pavilion
      • Small white stone fence functioning as bench on the line of the former fortification. Signed only on the groundlevel by white walkway where the small wall would be disfunctional or dangerous
    • Information and communication:
      • Website development: more written information online, less on the site.
      • Insdie the pavilion: small information tables about the visible view and about the history of the place fitted to the style of the pavilion






The idea of the new stairs

The present beton stairs leading up to the hill are demaged and non-aesthetic. Instead of them I advice a wooden-framed soil-filled stair. The treaps would be covered by white gravel. The natural material smoothly fits the grass of the hill, while the gravel cover helps the staps. The white color domintes the plan thanks to the closely situated registration of marriages. The wooden soil-filled and gravel covered stair is an environmental-friendly and economic solution.

Instead of the present direct route of the stair, I propose a spiral route around the hill. The spiral route would emphasize the feeling of having a full view around both the castle and the city; it helps avoiding reconstructing an original route to the castle; it disables the wrong use which leads to the erosion of the hill.

The first step is aligning the surface of the hill and balancing its slipped sides. If after the statical study it is necassary, it can be straigthened by a built ring on the bottom from the side of the Clare colony in order to avoid the further slipping. The Oxford Castle hill can be taken as an example for this work.

The idea of the pavilion

I suggest the building of a small look-out tower on the top of the hill. I would like to avoid the medieval-like towers or the ones which you can meet while hiking. Therefore, it would be designed like a gazebo in order to fulfil its desired aims. These are developping the view of the hill and from the hill; giving a place for gaining more information on the history of the castle and on the visible attractions; fitting into the present community use.

The small one-story building would be slightly elevated with a few steps for being able to look throught the Clare colony and the Saint Giles church. It would be a light-structured pavilion in order to avoid blocking the view.

It would be situated not in the middle of the hill, since there is a geodesic fix-point which cannot be covered.

Inside of it the information tables could take place.

For emphasizing the sense of community, I propose an invitation for tenders among the architecture students of Cambridge for planning the building.

The idea of the walkway and fence

The idea behind the "up to the past" slogen is the necessity to climbing up for being able to see the castle. The contour of the fortification could be signed by the combination of a white gravel walkway and small white walls functioning as benches where it is availabe and not covered. As a first step revisioning of the present vegetation is necessary.
